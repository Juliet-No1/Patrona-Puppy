Puppy Care

A full “puppy spa” means giving your dog a complete grooming session from head to paw. This can be done by you, a professional groomer, or even your vet. Grooming is not only about making your puppy look neat and cute but also a way to check if your puppy is healthy.

Bathing

Not sure how often to wash your puppy? It really depends on their habits! Bathing too often isn’t good, since it can dry out their skin and take away natural oils, leaving their coat dull and flaky.

FUsually, a bath is only needed if your pup is smelly, dirty, or has been rolling around in the mud. Remember – only use gentle shampoos made just for pets!.

Grooming

Puppy grooming involves consistent coat care through brushing and occasional trimming. Since a puppy’s coat differs from the adult coat—being softer, shorter, and fluffier—it may not require extensive maintenance at this stage. However, early introduction to grooming practices is crucial for long-term ease. Routine brushing stimulates blood circulation and, when done weekly, can effectively minimize shedding.

How important of cleaning puppy's ears

You may believe your dog doesn’t really need ear cleaning. After all, lots of dogs go through their entire lives without it. That’s not because their ears never need attention, but because many owners assume that animals just handle things by themselves.

The truth is, dogs are a lot like us—they can easily develop wax and dirt buildup in their ears. If no one steps in to help, it can get irritating, stir up allergies, and sometimes even lead to a nasty infection.

Check Dog's Eyes

Check your puppy’s eyes often – regular cleaning is recommended to keep the eye area clean from dust and dirt, especially if you have a breed that requires grooming around its face. The hair near their eyes can obscure vision and cause dirt to collect and tear ducts to block. Signs of an eye problem include eye discharge, redness and squinting or holding an eye closed. If you notice any eye changes take your pet to the local vet as soon as possible, as eye problems can worsen rapidly.

Cleaning Your Dogs Paws

Once your puppy starts walking on pavements, his nails will be trimmed naturally by walking on the hard surface. Regularly check the condition and length of his nails, including any ‘thumb’ nails, also known as ‘Dew claws’, higher up on the front and back feet.
